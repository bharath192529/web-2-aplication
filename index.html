virat

Virat Kohli, born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, is one of the most celebrated cricketers in the world. He grew up in a Punjabi family, where his father was a lawyer and his mother a homemaker. Virat showed an early passion for cricket, and at the age of nine, he joined the West Delhi Cricket Academy. Early Career Kohli's talent became evident during his teenage years. He made his debut for the Indian U-19 team in 2006 and led the team to victory in the 2008 U-19 World Cup. His aggressive batting style and determination caught the attention of selectors. International Debut Virat made his ODI debut against Sri Lanka in 2008 and quickly established himself as a reliable batsman. He made his Test debut in 2011 against the West Indies. Over the years, his performances earned him a reputation as one of the best batsmen in the world. Rise to Stardom Kohli's career took off with his aggressive approach and consistency. He played a crucial role in numerous victories for India across formats, particularly in chasing targets. In 2013, he was appointed vice-captain of the ODI team and later became the captain of the Test team in 2014. Records and Achievements Virat has broken multiple records, including being one of the fastest players to score 8,000, 9,000, and 10,000 runs in ODIs. He has numerous centuries in both ODIs and Tests and has led India to several significant victories, including a historic Test series win in Australia. Personal Life Off the field, Kohli married Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma in December 2017. The couple welcomed their daughter, Vamika, in January 2021. Kohli is known for his fitness regimen and has promoted various health and wellness initiatives. Philanthropy Beyond cricket, Virat is involved in various charitable activities and founded the Virat Kohli Foundation, which supports underprivileged children. Legacy Kohli is not only known for his cricketing skills but also for his passionate demeanor on the field. He has inspired a generation of cricketers and fans alike, and his impact on the game continues to be profound.
